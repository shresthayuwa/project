Nepal Passes "Clean Energy Promotion and Regulation Act, 2024

Kathmandu, October 20, 2024 - The Government of Nepal has officially passed the "Clean Energy Promotion and Regulation Act, 2024", approved by the Federal Parliament on October 18 and signed into law by President Bidya Devi Bhandari.

This law aims to enhance the use of renewable energy, including hydropower, solar, and wind, while aligning with international climate commitments. Minister Pampha Bhusal emphasized its importance for energy security and reducing fossil fuel reliance.

Key Provisions:

  1. Incentives: Tax exemptions and subsidies for clean energy projects, including a 50% income tax reduction for the first five years.
  2. Renewable Energy Quotas: By 2030, at least 50% of energy output from all electricity producers must come from renewable sources.
  3. National Clean Energy Fund: Established to support clean energy research and assist rural electrification.
  4. Regulatory Authority: A new body will oversee compliance and environmental standards for energy projects.
  5. Energy Efficiency Standards: New buildings and industries must meet minimum energy efficiency standards by 2026.
  6. Job Creation: Vocational training programs will be launched for skills in the renewable sector.

The law is effective immediately, with full implementation expected by January 1, 2025. It is anticipated to improve energy access, reduce costs for solar systems, and create jobs while positioning Nepal as a leader in South Asia's renewable energy landscape.
